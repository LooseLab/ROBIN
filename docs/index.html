ROBIN

Rapid nanopOre Brain intraoperatIve classificatioN

Currently in the UK, patients, families, and clinicians wait on average 6 weeks for a complete molecular profile of a CNS tumour, delaying treatment decisions and causing significant uncertainty.

ROBIN, and other related approaches, provide a solution that can reduce that wait time down to mere hours, enabling equitable access to rapid CNS tumour diagnostics for all patients, regardless of their location or circumstances.

This results in faster access to appropriate treatments and trials, reduces uncertainity for patients, families and clinicians, and would dramatically reduce the overall cost of care.

About ROBIN

ROBIN is a powerful real-time bioinformatics interface designed for oncology applications utilising Oxford Nanopore Technologies (ONT) sequencers.

It provides a comprehensive suite of tools for analyzing genomic data, with a focus on real-time analysis during DNA sequencing runs.

This approach allows pathologists to reports results to surgeons and clinicians in the shortest possible time, allowing for faster treatment decisions and reducing uncertainty for patients, families and clinicians.

In many cases, surgeons can have confirmation of the tumour type and grade before the patient has even left the operating theatre.

Currently ROBIN is research use only, but working closely with clinicians and surgeons in the UK we hope to be able to make these methods available across the UK and beyond as soon as possible.
